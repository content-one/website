How it works

Content.One pairs your content models with audience rules, signals, and lightweight AI.

Audience rules, content variants, real-time decisioning, and AI assist render the best-fit experience at request time.

01

Audience rules

Segment by UTM, referrer, device, location, language, visit history, campaign, or custom traits.

02

Content variants

Create multiple versions of hero blocks, headlines, CTAs, or pricing modules and map them to segments.

03

Real-time decisioning

Server-side logic chooses the right variant per request for speed, SEO, and clear analytics.

04

AI assist

Generate on-brand copy suggestions and tone tweaks per audience, then approve with one click.

05

Preview & share links

06

Governed workflows

Role-based approvals ensure the right people can create segments, launch tests, and publish variants.

Use the signals you already have

Content.One accepts zero/first-party, contextual, campaign, and optional commerce/CRM signals—while honoring your data governance.

  • Zero/First-party: Form responses, quiz outcomes, preferences, on-site behavior.
  • Contextual: Geolocation, language, device type, time-of-day, entry page, referrer.
  • Campaign: UTM parameters, ad group, organic vs. paid, email links.
  • Commerce/CRM: Integrate customer tags or lifecycle stages via API when needed.
Performance & SEO friendly

Server-side variants keep pages fast and crawlable

  • Analytics label exposures and conversions clearly for each variant.

Governance, privacy & control

Respect for users and compliance is built into Content.One personalization.

  • Consent-aware: Honor consent states and degrade gracefully to contextual rules when needed.
  • Role-based access: Limit who can create segments, launch tests, or publish variants.
  • Audit trails: Track who changed what and when for each variant and rule.
  • Data minimization: Use only necessary signals; integrate securely when passing identifiers.

Common use cases

  • Geo-aware offers: Show regional pricing, inventory, or shipping timelines.
  • Lifecycle messaging: Swap CTAs for new vs. returning visitors or CRM stage.
  • Campaign continuity: Align landing page hero with ad creative via UTM rules.
  • Industry personas: Tailor case studies, logos, and benefits for each vertical.
  • Language preference: Auto-serve copy and media for the user’s preferred language.

Getting started

  1. Define segments: Start with simple rules (location, device, UTM) and add custom traits over time.
  2. Create variants: Add targeted copy, imagery, and CTAs to the components that matter most.
  3. Preview & QA: Use segment preview links to validate experiences end-to-end.
  4. Publish & measure: Launch variants, track goals, and iterate based on automated summaries.
